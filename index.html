Technology Vision Statement

My background for using technology in the classroom involves a lot of training and experience with successful student achievement. I have been trained on using a variety of technology resources including Desmos, Peardeck, and Quizziz. I also use a Smartboard on a daily basis to project math problems, videos, and graphs. It allows me to easily write next to the problem, and graph equations. I also started using Khan Academy in my class a couple years ago and my students have definitely had more achievement using this resource. It provides a lot of intervention or enrichment problems and has videos/hints for each problem if my students need it. My students also can work on Khan Academy missions which help them master the entire grade level math they are in. Using these resources and seeing my students succeed has helped developed my technology vision. I believe technology is an amazing tool that needs to be used in classrooms on a daily basis. Technology should be used to help create engaging and fun lessons for students. For example, "Effectively applied content neutral technologies increase student's access to information and ideas and enhance student to student and student to teacher interactions to support and enrich sense making." For students to learn be successful as 21st century learners, they did to know how to use technology effectively and creatively to solve real-world problems. Teachers need to teach using technology resources to prepare students to be successful in their future careers. This can be supported by "Findings from a number of studies have shown that the strategic use of technological tools can support both the learning of mathematical procedures and skills as well as the development of advanced mathematical proficiencies, such as problem solving, reasoning, and justifying" ("Strategic Use of Technology," 2015). My goal as an educator is to teach students how to use technology safely. I want to use technology to differentiate instruction on a daily basis and also provide fun, engaging lessons the students will relate to. Teachers need to teach students how to be critical thinkers and how to collaborate using technology as well.

Source: Strategic Use of Technology in Teaching and Learning Mathematics. (2015, July). Retrieved September 22, 2019, from https://www.nctm.org/Standards-and-Positions/Position-Statements/ Strategic-Use-of-Technology-in-Teaching-and-Learning-Mathematics/
